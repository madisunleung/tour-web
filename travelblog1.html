Day Trip to Stanley

October 1, 2021

Visit the seaside village with a laid-back vibe, popular for day trips. Dine at eateries and cafes line the promenade on Stanley Bay.

Top 3 places to visit in Stanley:

  • Si Yik Cha Chaan Teng
  • Hong Kong Correctional Services Museum
  • Stanley Market

Si Yik Cha Chaan Teng

Once you arrive at Stanley market, head over to Si Yik Cha Chaan Teng for lunch. The restaurants serves noodle dishes and desserts ranging from kaya French toast to pineapple bun with butter. It is best to pair with Yuenyeung, a beverage made with three parts coffee and seven parts milk tea. The French toast sold out very fast so make sure to arrive early. The shop hides in a corner so make sure to look out for it carefully.

Hong Kong Correctional Services Museum

The Stanley prison was established in 1937. The Hong Kong Correctional Services Museum provides informative and visual displays to demonstrate the history and development of prison service in Hong Kong. The museum closes for cleaning from 1 -2 PM and closes at 5 PM.

Stanley Market

There are many shopping stalls in Stanley market. You can find an array of little shops selling silk garments, sportswear, art, Chinese costume jewelry and souvenirs. You can also walk along the bay and spot the Black Pier as well as the Murray House.

Travel by bus

The public bus routes operated by CityBus and New World First Bus (NWFB) are fast, convenient and cheap. With modern double decker air conditioned busses they are very comfortable, and if you take a seat at the front of the top deck they give an excellent sightseeing view as well.

Day Trip in San Po Kong

September 15, 2021

Located between Wong Tai Sin and Diamond Hill. This compact grid of industrial and residential buildings has become a great spot for eateries and more.

Top 3 places to visit in San Po Kong:

  • King of Soyabeans
  • Bleak House Books
  • Chi Lin Nunnery and Nan Lian Garden

King of Soyabeans

This restaurant is notorious for its freshly made Shanghainese sticky rice rolls, and soy milk as well other bean curd snacks. Besides, it offers a variety of dim sum which come with an affordable price. On top of that, it is listed in a Michelin guide of Hong Kong.

Bleak House Books

Hidden in an industrial building, Bleak House is a cozy, small bookstore in San Po Kong. It offers a wide range of non-fiction and fictional books. Apart from those, it also sells second-hand book at an affordable price as well as goods from local artist. Get yourself ready to become a bookworm.

Chi Lin Nunnery and Nan Lian Garden

Chi Lin Nunnery is a large Buddhist temple complex. It was founded in 1934 as a retreat for Buddhist nuns and was rebuilt in the 1998 following the traditional Tang Dynasty architecture. Nan Lian Garden is located near Chi Lin Nunnery. It is the only Tang landscape left. This landmark surely is a hidden gem in the middle of the residential areas like Diamond Hill.
